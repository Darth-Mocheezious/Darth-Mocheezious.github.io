What And How Did Muslims Contribute To Science?

Intro

The Islamic Golden Age, which scholars say lasted from around the 8th century to the 14th century. During this period, many scientific advances and discoveries were made by Muslims.

Astronomy and Astrophysics

Astronomy was a very important thing to Muslims; Muslims used the Sun to tell time and to know when to pray, and used the moon to know when the months began/ended. The astrolabe was made in ancient Greece, but Muslims scientists made it better. Muslim scientists also discovered many of the stars and constellations.

Physiology and Anatomy

Muslim scientists contributed greatly to anatomy/physiology. Scientist Ibn Al-Nafis theorized that capillaries exist to connect veins and arteries, a prediction which was proven true about 400 years later. Al-Jahiz's works were used to develop the theories of evolution and natural selection. Muslim scientists theorized about the nervous system and their theories were mostly true.

Geography and Geology

Muslim scientist Al-Biruni made the most accurate estimate of the Earth's radius; 6339.6 km. Famous cartographer Al-Idrisi made the most accurate world map at the time, and was invited to many courts to share his maps. Muslim scientists said that the Earth was not flat, which has been proven correct

Math

Muslims invented the symbol for 0. Al-Kharazimi is credited with the father of algebra. Muslim mathematicians made many maths. Muslims contributed greatly to geometry, trignometry, and calculus.

Medicine

The very first Muslim hospital was in built in Baghdad in 805. Muslims doctors and scientists discovered new diseases, viruses, cures, etc. Muslims were the ones who discovered the difference between smallpox and measles. Muslims also started the practice of surgery and imporved upon the practice. Abi Sina's book The Canon of Medicine was the most popular and reliable book on medicines and ailments and their treatments and crues at the time.
